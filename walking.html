“The lot is cast into the lap, but its every decision is from the Lord.”
Proverbs 16:33

This verse is kind of mind boggling to me sometimes but reassuring in its own way. The reason I found it mind boggling was because it implies that things can happen by chance but God is in charge of that chance. I think it is reassuring though because it means even something as random as chance is intentional and sincere from God.

~ Lois Nfor, Northeastern ~

"Therefore, since we are surrounded by so great a cloud of witnesses, let us also lay aside every weight, and sin which clings so closely, and let us run with endurance the race that is set before us, looking to Jesus, the founder and perfecter of our faith, who for the joy that was set before him endured the cross, despising the shame, and is seated at the right hand of the throne of God."
Hebrews 12:1-2

There are many times where I fell short of my own expectations of my faith, or became overwhelmed by the an idea that my faith was something I owned and had to grow by trying harder or simply being better. But this verse encourages me to keep running, to lay aside those difficult sins and weights not simply because I want to better myself, but because of Jesus, who suffered and died with joy for me. The verse also encourages me that I am not alone. Jesus is the founder of my faith, and he will continue to be there to guide me, and to perfect me.

~ Tim Kim, Boston University ~

I think of this verse almost every time I run (literally). Throughout college, there were definitely many moments when I felt weary and weak. But this verse always reminds me not to lean on my own understanding or strength, but to look to JESUS--the founder and perfector of our faith. He is the reason we run this spiritual race, and He is the one who will renew our strength. We need endurance to run this race, and it doesn't come overnight. It comes from continually coming to Jesus and saying "I need you." I hope this verse brings hope and comfort to anyone who needs a reminder of WHY and HOW we can persevere together as followers of Christ

~ Soomin Lee, Boston University ~

“I have set the Lord always before me; because he is at my right hand, I shall not be shaken."
Psalm 16:8

Keeping the Lord by our side ensures us that we are secure, walking with Him, as we traverse through our own lives. He wants YOU to know that, He wants YOU to trust in Him. Through every season, through the turmoil, the joyous times, the Lord will always be there. Right next to you.

~ Shangyin (Sunyin) Au, Boston University ~
